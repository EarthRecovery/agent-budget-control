### Main Takeaways From Table 1

- `Claude Opus 4.7 Low Thinking` is the strongest **rollout** model on both benchmarks.
  - `SearchR1`: `75.8%`
  - `Sokoban`: `56.2%`
- The strongest rollout model is not always the strongest **estimator**.
  - On `SearchR1`, `Claude Sonnet 4.6 Low Thinking` is stronger on successful-rollout estimation metrics.
  - On `Sokoban`, `GPT-5.2 Instant` is stronger on successful-rollout estimation metrics.
- `GPT-5.2 Instant` produces many more estimation samples on both tasks, which is consistent with longer rollouts and higher average turn counts.
- Estimation behavior differs by benchmark.
  - `SearchR1` is generally more optimistic.
  - `Sokoban` is generally more conservative.
- Cache usage differs clearly by provider.
  - `GPT-5.2 Instant` shows visible cached-token usage.
  - The two Claude low-thinking variants show almost no cache hits in the current setup.

## Final Conclusion

- Strongest **rollout** model: `Claude Opus 4.7 Low Thinking` on both benchmarks.
- Strongest **estimator**:
  - `SearchR1`: `Claude Sonnet 4.6 Low Thinking`
  - `Sokoban`: `GPT-5.2 Instant`
- The main conclusion is not that one model dominates everything.
- The more important conclusion is that the **actor** and the **estimator** should be evaluated separately.
